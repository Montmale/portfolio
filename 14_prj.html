→ a research thesis exploring visual dissent today through Adorno's aesthetics of dissonance

About

Design is not a neutral language, but a political and social act capable of shaping reality and reinforcing power hierarchies through the aesthetics of perfection. This thesis explores the concept of dissonant aesthetics as a tool of critical resistance against “imposed beauty” and the logic of Capitalist Realism.

The analysis traces the historical genealogies of visual dissent, from punk to the avant-garde, up to contemporary manifestations such as glitch art and memes. The research investigates whether and how these forms of rupture can survive in a digital ecosystem that tends to neutralize diversity by turning it into merchandise.

The ultimate goal is to define a theoretical framework for identifying visual “free zones” where design can still generate alternative narratives and critical thinking.
