→ an exploration of the aesthetic and cultural depth of Japanese writing systems

About

This booklet explores the core of Japanese typography, examining its aesthetic richness and cultural significance. It highlights the three writing systems—kanji, hiragana, and katakana—and their enduring impact on modern design.

Inspired by Kenya Hara’s philosophy, the concept of emptiness influences both the content and the layout. The design evokes a sense of lightness and elegance, reflecting the purity of Japanese characters, while the minimalist palette pays homage to traditional craftsmanship.

More than just a publication, this issue is a conceptual and visual journey through the graphic language of Japan.
